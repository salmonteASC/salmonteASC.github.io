Suicide Prevention


Ask For Help

Teenage suicide can be prevented/lowered in a few ways. These ways are asking for help, lowering the amount of ignorance, and psychotherapy. Asking for help is a great way to prevent suicide. This is because people can help you overcome the problems you are dealing with. Suicide might seem like the easiest solutions to family and personal problems, but these problems can be overcome with the help of others. People who feel like committing suicide shouldnâ€™t be afraid to ask for help(National Suicide Prevention Lifeline). This can help people erase the thoughts of committing suicide.

Raise Awareness

Lowering the amount of ignorance also helps with preventing/lowering teenage suicide rates. People can be less ignorant by being educated on suicide. Parents should also be aware of any family history of mental illness and they should talk to their children about it. Also if a teenager makes a threat about wanting to harm themselves, it should be taken seriously. This helps lower the amount of ignorance and in return, it lowers the rate of teenage suicide.

You are NOT Alone

If you or a friend may be in danger, make sure to speak to someone and use available resources to prevent suicide from happening.
